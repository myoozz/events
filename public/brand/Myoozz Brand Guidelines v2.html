§ 01 · Foundation

Built on craft. Bent toward what's next.

Three commitments shape every decision in this document. Read them once; they sit underneath every page that follows.

Commitment 01

We carry the craft forward.

Years of running rooms, planning campaigns, and watching customers tell us what's broken. We take what works and bring it to the next medium — software, automation, AI — without losing the manners.

Commitment 02

We fill the gaps quietly.

Our products start as observations: a customer chasing leads at midnight, a colleague rebuilding the same deck for the third time. Look closely and you'll see we're solving the things nobody else got around to fixing.

Commitment 03

A system, not a style.

Sub-brands plug in by following the M-second-letter rule. Color, naming, type and the bubbles do the rest. We never start from scratch — and the next person on the team won't either.

Mission

Take what marketing has taught us, hand it to technology, and leave a better way of working for everyone who comes next.

§ 02 · The mark

Locked artwork. Two masters. One M.

The MYOOZZ wordmark is registered trademark artwork — never re-typeset, never redrawn. There are exactly two masters: the original red mark (transparent background) for use on light surfaces, and a white master for use on red and ink. The standalone M is the only place we still use Poppins Bold by hand.

Primary — red mark on cream

The trademarked artwork, transparent background. Place on cream, white, off-white, or any photo that's been muted to a flat plate. Never sit it on a white rectangle — let the mark breathe on the surface itself.

Reverse — white mark on red

For Myoozz Red, ink, and dark photography. Use the white master directly — never CSS-filter the red original.

Monogram — M

Custom-drawn M derived from Poppins Black, optically corrected. Used at small sizes & as a quiet stamp. The M is artwork — never re-typeset.

Bubble glyph

Favicon, app icon, profile picture, loading state. The shape alone is enough.

Stacked lockup

Vertical real estate — social avatars, square placements, badges.

Clear space & minimums
x
x

Clear space = x (cap-height of the M). Minimum digital width: 96px for full wordmark, 24px for monogram. Print: 20mm wordmark, 6mm monogram.

Don't
No re-typesetting

The wordmark is locked TM artwork. Never rebuild it from a font.

No white plate

The red mark sits on the surface — no white box behind it.

No stretching

Don't condense, expand, rotate, italicize, or skew.

No re-color

Only the supplied red, white, and ink masters. No gradients.

§ 03 · Color

Red is the master. Sub-brands wear their own color.

Myoozz Red belongs to the parent brand. Each sub-brand owns one color used as a field, not an accent — the locked M monogram and the second letter both sit white on top, and the chosen hue fills the surface behind. This inverts the noise: less red overall, more visual identity per product.

§ 04 · Typography

Three families. Each does one job.

The M monogram is custom artwork (a redrawn Poppins Black M, optically corrected) and is never re-typeset — always use the supplied PNG/SVG. Bricolage Grotesque is the architectural voice: headlines, statements, section heads. Inter handles every other word: body, UI, captions, labels, fine print. JetBrains Mono is reserved for system signals — receipts, codes, IDs, technical labels.

§ 05 · Sub-brand framework

M leads. Letter follows. Colored field.

Every Myoozz product reads as M + lowercase letter — both white, on the product's color field. Each pair (Me, Mn, Ma, Mx) is supplied as locked artwork, kerned and optically corrected — never re-typeset, never rebuilt from a font. The family resemblance comes from the M; the product comes from the letter and the hue.

Primary lockups — locked artwork, one per product
M·E EVENTS

Today's hero. Production, on-ground, IPs.

M·N NEXUS

Lead-data & CRM engine. (Note: amber field — pending realignment to mn-blue.)

M·A ASSISTANT

WhatsApp-native agent. Green pairs with the channel.

M·X EXPERIMENTS

Sandbox, R&D, anything pre-launch.

Secondary mark — single letter with M flag

For surfaces where the full M-letter pair is too tight — favicons, app icons, social avatars at small sizes, sticker sheets — use the single-letter form with M as a small superscript flag (eM). The letter does the work; the M flag preserves family identity. Never use this as the primary lockup — the wordmark or the M-letter pair must lead.

How to add a new product
  1. Pick a free single letter (B, C, D, F, G, H, I, J, K, L, O, P, Q, R, S, T, U, V, W, Y, Z).
  2. Choose a hue from the reserved palette (right). Avoid clashes with existing sub-brand colors.
  3. Lockup is the M monogram (locked artwork, white) + your letter (Fraunces italic, white) on the chosen color.
  4. Master brand stays Myoozz in red. Your product never replaces it — it joins the family.
  5. Domain pattern: myoozz.[product] (e.g. myoozz.events).

§ 06 · Voice

Sound like a senior practitioner who's done the work.

Myoozz speaks the way a good operator briefs a teammate — direct without being curt, warm without being eager, certain without performing. We earn authority by what we've already shipped, not by claiming it.

Observed, not assumed.
Say"We watched ten event teams chase leads after the show. So we built Ma."
Not"Revolutionary AI-powered lead reactivation engine."
Warm, not cute.
Say"Hey — we built this so you don't have to chase leads at 11pm."
Not"Meet your new bestie 🤖✨ Ma is here to slay."
Certain, not boastful.
Say"Six weeks. Four cities. 12,400 leads replied to."
Not"India's #1 award-winning event powerhouse leader."
§ 07 · Environments

Mostly online. Offline only where we control the room.

The brand lives almost entirely on screens — site, social, decks, ads, email, dashboards. Offline applications are limited to office stationery and boards/signage in spaces we control. We don't print billboards, OOH, or third-party merch unless explicitly briefed.
